About the Researcher

I'm Tokalo Bi Tokalo Armel, an engineering student at EMINES - School of Industrial Management, University Mohammed VI Polytechnic (UM6P). My research focuses on sustainable technologies that address critical environmental challenges while considering industrial applicability.

The BARTHx Corporation project emerged from my work in UM6P's Materials Science and Nanotechnology (MSN) laboratories, where I explored innovative approaches to carbon capture. This technology represents a practical solution to a dual challenge: reducing atmospheric CO₂ levels while protecting workers in high-emission environments.

Technology in Detail

How BARTHx Works

1

Gas Intake

CO₂-rich emissions enter the system through specialized intake ports.

2

Chemical Adsorption

Proprietary Solution X selectively captures CO₂ molecules through chemical adsorption.

3

Intermediate Storage

Captured CO₂ is stored in stable form within the adsorbent matrix.

4

Thermal Activation

Controlled heating releases concentrated CO₂ for recovery or utilization.

Prototype Application: Automotive Sector

The automotive prototype integrates with existing exhaust treatment systems (SCR and particulate filters) to capture CO₂ produced during NOx reduction. This represents a breakthrough in vehicle emissions control.

  • Compatible with existing vehicle systems
  • No modification to engine operation required
  • Modular design for scalability
  • Suitable for both light and heavy vehicles
